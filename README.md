# Tracking the Evolution of the Loss and Damage Financing Architecture under the UNFCCC

**Author:** K R Hari Krishna  
**Institution:** Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai  

## Overview

This repository contains the research manuscript for the thesis titled **"Tracking the Evolution of the Loss and Damage Financing Architecture under the UNFCCC"**. 

The study tracks the development of the concept of Loss and Damage (L&D) within the UNFCCC and assesses the extent to which the shift from "liability and compensation" to "cooperation and facilitation" structurally limits the Fund for Loss and Damage (FRLD)'s potential to mobilise revenue.

## Abstract

Loss and damage (L&D) was central to climate talks for more than two decades before being formally set up in the Warsaw International Mechanism (2013) and Article 8 of the Paris Agreement (2015). Using Fairclough's three-part model for critical discourse analysis, this thesis examines Decision 2/CP.19 and how it alters the power dynamic between developed and developing nations under the UNFCCC.
